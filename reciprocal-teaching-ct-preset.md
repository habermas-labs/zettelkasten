## Origin

Palincsar and Brown (1984) developed Reciprocal Teaching as a cooperative reading comprehension strategy grounded in Vygotsky's zone of proximal development and the apprenticeship model. Students work in groups of four, each holding one of four roles that correspond to key comprehension strategies. Roles rotate so every participant eventually holds every position.

The connection to Crosstalk surfaced not through research but through a spontaneous cognitive collision — a thread about contacting an educational researcher snapped into alignment with CT's four-vertex structure mid-thought.

---

## The Four Roles

**Predictor** — anticipates what comes next. In reading comprehension: uses title, headings, and prior content to make informed predictions. In CT triangulation: anticipates where the argument is heading, what implications follow from current positions, what the next productive question is. A forward-looking epistemic role not previously named in the CT taxonomy.

**Questioner** — generates questions about the material. Asks about key ideas, confusing points, implications. Encourages deeper thinking. Distinct from the Critical Reader role in CT — the Questioner is generative and opening, the Critical Reader is adversarial and pressure-testing. Both are valuable; they serve different epistemic moments.

**Clarifier** — identifies where understanding has broken down and repairs it. Flags difficult concepts, suggests reframing, resolves ambiguity. A role not previously in the CT preset library — the model whose job is comprehension repair rather than generation or critique.

**Summarizer** — condenses main ideas in the group's own words. Identifies the central claim and supporting structure. Maps onto the Synthesizer role already in CT's taxonomy.

---

## Mapping to Crosstalk's Four-Vertex Structure

The fit with CT's tetrahedron concept is structural, not approximate:

| Vertex | Role | CT function |
|---|---|---|
| Model A | Predictor | Forward-looking; anticipates implications |
| Model B | Questioner | Generative inquiry; opens rather than closes |
| Model C | Clarifier | Comprehension repair; identifies breakdown |
| Human conductor | Summarizer / Rotator | Consolidates; directs; holds the session |

The human as fourth vertex mirrors the teacher role in Palincsar's original design — the teacher initially models all four strategies, then gradually releases responsibility to students. In CT the human conductor holds the meta-level view, directs attention, and decides what to pursue. The roles rotate in reciprocal teaching so every student internalizes every strategy; in CT the human could rotate role assignments across sessions for the same reason.

## The Vygotsky–Bakhtin Connection

Palincsar worked explicitly from Vygotsky's ZPD and apprenticeship model. Vygotsky and Bakhtin were contemporaries working in overlapping Soviet intellectual territory — both concerned with how meaning emerges through social interaction rather than inside individual minds.

The ZPD (what a learner can do with support that they cannot yet do alone) and outsideness/vnenakhodimost' (what a listener perceives about the speaker that the speaker cannot see from within) are structural cousins. Both locate the generative moment at the boundary between participants rather than inside any one of them.

This means Reciprocal Teaching and Crosstalk's triangulation philosophy share a common theoretical ancestor. The preset isn't borrowed from education — it's a convergent development from the same underlying epistemology.

---

## What This Adds to the CT Role Taxonomy

Two roles from Reciprocal Teaching are genuinely new to the CT preset library:

**Clarifier** — no existing CT role is specifically tasked with identifying comprehension breakdown and repairing it. The Critical Reader pressure-tests; the Observer watches the shape of the conversation; neither is focused on repair. Clarifier fills that gap.

**Predictor** — no existing CT role is explicitly forward-looking. The Synthesizer consolidates what has been established; the Predictor anticipates what follows. In a long triangulation session the Predictor role keeps the session from becoming purely retrospective.

The Questioner partially overlaps with the Critical Reader but is worth distinguishing in the preset library — the difference between opening inquiry and adversarial testing is meaningful and should be available as a named choice.

---

## Educational Resonance

This preset has an obvious application beyond general CT use: it is a ready-made framework for using Crosstalk as an educational tool. A student or teacher bringing a complex text, argument, or problem into CT could assign the four roles explicitly and run a session structured exactly as Palincsar intended — with AI models holding three of the four positions and the human holding the fourth.

The role rotation principle from reciprocal teaching suggests a session design where role assignments shift across turns or sessions, ensuring the human conductor experiences each epistemic position rather than permanently holding the meta-level view.
