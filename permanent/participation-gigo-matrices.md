## Two Instruments, Not One Framework

These are two distinct analytical tools that operate at different levels and can be composed but should not be conflated. The participation mode matrix is a typology — it describes structural configurations of engagement. The GIGO matrix is a heuristic — a calibratable lens for reading the epistemic character of what an interaction is producing. They are orthogonal: you can apply GIGO to any cell in the participation matrix, and you can observe a GIGO dynamic and ask which participation mode it's occurring within.

The relationship between them is addressed in a dedicated section below.

---

## The GIGO Matrix

GIGO reframed: not a warning about contaminated input but a quality characterization in both directions simultaneously. Garbage and Gem are the poles of a quality axis applied to both human input and AI output. The four cells describe the character of the interaction as a whole, not just AI performance.

Human is placed on the y-axis because the human is the initiating variable — the human determines which row; the AI determines which column. Only the human can change rows.

|  | **AI: Garbage Out** | **AI: Gem Out** |
|---|---|---|
| **Human: Gem In** | | |
| **Human: Garbage In** | | |

**The cells are intentionally blank.** This is a design decision, not an incompleteness. The content of each cell is context-dependent — what counts as garbage or gem in a given interaction is determined by the stakes, the purpose, and what the interaction is trying to produce. Filling the cells prescriptively would defeat the instrument's purpose by pre-loading the judgment it is designed to generate. The analyst brings the context; the matrix holds the frame.

**Calibration scales with stakes.** For low-stakes interactions, quality is the appropriate read: did this produce a good enough result? For high-stakes interactions, the epistemic direction reading becomes more load-bearing: is this interaction opening or closing the knowledge space? Is it producing or collapsing epistemic range — the diversity/monoculture tension that EoB tracks? The same matrix, calibrated differently. The analyst decides which reading the situation demands.

## The Participation Mode Matrix

Three participation modes, not two. Passive is a participation mode, not non-participation — consistent with EoB: you cannot not participate. Active participation can be give-and-give without genuine exchange. Generative participation requires interaction and iteration — this is RIFF.

**Participation mode characterizations:**
- Passive: chosen engagement at minimal intensity — a cell, not an absence; the appearance of non-participation is itself a participation choice
- Active: effort directed into the exchange — give-and-give is possible here without genuine reception; the most common mode; can be the most mindless form of RIP
- Generative: interaction AND iteration — taking input, reflecting it against what you own, interrogating both views, folding input forward; this is RIFF as a participation mode

| | **AI: Passive** | **AI: Active** | **AI: Generative** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Human: Passive** | Settling | Collecting | Sampling |
| **Human: Active** | Pushing | Exchanging | Selecting |
| **Human: Generative** | Leading | Stretching | Collaborating |

**Cell labels are participation-anchored.** Each name describes what the human is doing in that configuration — not what outcome the configuration produces, not whether the configuration is desirable. The label carries no inherent evaluative charge. The same cell can describe a good or poor outcome depending on context. EoB reads the context, not the cell.

**Reading the gradients:**
- Horizontally: as AI engagement increases, the human's activity shifts from driving unilaterally (Pushing, Leading) toward receiving more of what the AI offers (Selecting, Stretching) toward genuine mutual generation (Collaborating)
- Vertically: as human engagement increases, the same AI mode produces qualitatively different participation — Collecting becomes Exchanging becomes Stretching as the human brings more
- The gradients are orthogonal, which is what makes the off-diagonal cells analytically interesting: different imbalances have different characters, not just different magnitudes

## The Evolution of Cell Labels: Why Naming Matters

The participation matrix went through two earlier naming passes before arriving at the participation-anchored version above. Preserving those iterations is not mere provenance — they demonstrate in miniature the exact problem the matrix is designed to surface.

**Pass 1 — Evaluative (Claude-origin):**

| | Passive AI | Active AI | Generative AI |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Passive Human** | Stagnant | Spoon-fed | Unrequited |
| **Active Human** | Underserved | Transactional | Asymmetric |
| **Generative Human** | Squandered | Asymmetric | Synergistic |

Claude's language is strongly evaluative. Stagnant, Spoon-fed, Unrequited, Underserved, Squandered all imply deficiency, waste, or imbalance. Only Synergistic is clearly positive, creating a moral gravity toward Generative/Generative that the matrix's design explicitly rejects. The Generative Human row reads as a quality ladder. "Asymmetric" appears twice — directionally correct but collapsing a meaningful difference in who is ahead. This naming pass reflects the evaluative frame the analyst brings, not the structure of the matrix itself.

**Pass 2 — Directional (iterated with ChatGPT):**

| | Passive AI | Active AI | Generative AI |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Passive Human** | Disengaged | Receiving | Declining |
| **Active Human** | Investing | Exchanging | Overlooking |
| **Generative Human** | Outpacing | Reaching | Synergistic |

Improvement: the Asymmetric duplication is resolved. Overlooking and Reaching correctly capture directional asymmetry — the human missing something the AI is offering vs. the human reaching beyond what AI is matching. But evaluative shadow persists. Declining implies the human should be doing something different. Disengaged carries residual negative connotation. Synergistic retains its normative charge. The names are describing the gap between human and AI participation levels, not what the human is actually doing. Relational description still edges toward judgment.

**Pass 3 — Participation-anchored (current):**

The shift is to anchor names on the human's mode of engagement in that cell. Settling is not a failure to rise — it is efficient recognition that the task is done. Pushing is not a deficiency in the AI — it is the human extending effort into a low-response space, which may be exactly right for a drafting or exploration task. The name holds open both the good and poor outcome readings rather than pre-loading one.

**What the evolution shows:** The demeanor of a naming scheme — its affective and judgmental loading — shapes how the instrument gets used before any analysis has begun. Evaluative names build a ladder; participation-anchored names build a map. The analyst who picks up the evaluative version is already being steered toward Generative/Generative before they have read a single cell. The evolution of the labels enacts the argument about contextual grounding: the instrument should be calibrated by the analyst's context, not by the vocabulary choices embedded in its construction.

## Critical Framing

**No cell has inherent value.** The matrix is not a progress ladder. A passive/passive hot dog recipe request that returns one good option and stops — that is a good outcome. The participation mode matched the need. Settling quickly is sometimes the right move.

**Heat map as fit, not intensity.** The peak moves based on desired outcomes, task stakes, and the character of the interaction. A low-stakes task has a low, broad peak — it naturally spreads into neighboring cells without that being a failure. The AI returning three recipe options and asking narrowing questions, the human seeing something close and asking for variations — activity creeps in on both sides, and that is fine. A high-stakes task has a tall, narrow peak — spread there is a signal worth examining, because it may indicate underinvestment or a mismatch between what the task requires and what the participation mode is delivering.

Input brings the heat; output feels the heat. The human axis is where energy enters the system.

**The diagonal is not the most interesting.** The off-diagonal cells are where the analytical work lives. Human generative / AI active is CT's sweet spot for RIP, and simultaneously the highest sycophancy risk: the human is maximally invested, ego is maximally engaged, and the AI is structurally incentivized to calibrate toward that investment rather than toward epistemic quality. Human active / AI generative is a different configuration — the conductor shaping conditions for generation without generating the content; ego investment lives in the process architecture rather than the content, which is a safer epistemic location.

## Failure Mode Location Argument

Research on AI failure modes consistently situates the failure in the AI — sycophancy, hallucination, and bias are framed as AI output problems. The human participation pattern as a locus of failure is largely invisible. The tool-frame forecloses the question before it can be asked: if AI is a hammer, you examine the hammer when something goes wrong.

The participation matrix makes the human axis visible as an analytical variable. This reframes the failure mode question: where in the matrix does sycophancy live? In the cells where human ego investment is highest and the AI is in active or generative response mode — the AI is structurally incentivized to calibrate its response to the human's evident investment rather than to epistemic quality.

If sycophancy is solved programmatically rather than through human participation, the failure mode locus must be addressed as part of the solution. CT's model-to-model evaluation removes the ego-investment variable from the evaluative loop rather than training the AI to resist it. This is a structural intervention, not a behavioral patch.

## How the Two Matrices Work Together

The participation typology and the GIGO heuristic are complementary instruments operating at different levels:

**Participation typology** describes the structural configuration of the engagement — who is doing what, at what intensity, with what kind of epistemic motion. It is a map. It has nine cells. It tells you the shape of the interaction.

**GIGO heuristic** is a calibratable quality/epistemic lens applied to what the interaction is producing. It is coarse-grained and fast — designed for quick application mid-session or as a prompt check. It asks: what is the epistemic character of what is flowing in each direction?

EoB operates by bringing the GIGO lens (and other instruments) to a participation configuration to read what that configuration is actually producing. The diagnosis lives in the combination: what configuration are we in, and what is that configuration producing given the stakes and context?

The heat map topology connects both. For low-stakes tasks, GIGO reads quality — did this produce something good enough? The participation topology identifies which cells are active, which spread is natural, whether the mode fits. For high-stakes tasks, GIGO reads epistemic direction — is the interaction opening or collapsing the knowledge space? Is it tracking the diversity/monoculture tension? The participation topology identifies where ego investment is concentrated, where sycophancy risk is elevated, whether the mode is calibrated to what the task actually demands.

**The relativism question.** Leaving the GIGO cells blank and anchoring participation labels on what the human is doing rather than on outcome value can appear to rush toward relativism — as if all configurations are equally valid and context is just an excuse for anything. This reading inverts the actual demand. Both design choices push the analyst back toward grounding rather than away from it. You cannot hand-wave context away by citing a favorable cell name. The context must do the work; the matrix does not do it for you. This is a more demanding standard, not a more permissive one. Stepping out of a context and calling the resulting flexibility "relativism" simply ignores the grounding that real analytical work requires.

## Instrument Terminology

**Participation mode matrix = Typology.** Non-hierarchical, configurational, maps relational stances between actors. "Typology" is the correct term because the matrix describes patterned configurations, not developmental ranking. "Taxonomy" invites hierarchy questions ("which is most advanced?") that break the design intent.

**GIGO matrix = Heuristic.** Coarse-grained, fast, usable mid-action — the sort of thing you pause eight seconds to apply before sending a prompt. "Framework" is too large; "typology" would be wrong. The functional difference is the point: one slows thinking (typology, analytic, high-resolution); the other speeds it up (heuristic, operational, low-resolution). Forcing symmetry would flatten their different purposes.

## Origin: The 3×3

The participation mode matrix began as a 2×2 (human × AI, passive vs. active) and migrated to 3×3 through engagement with Bakhtin. Purely active participation without reception is a univocal voice — give-and-give without genuine exchange. Purely receptive is passive. The integration of receptiveness and expressiveness — taking something in and pushing it forward transformed — is generative, aligned with Bakhtin's multivocal, dialogic voice. The move to three modes was a philosophical decision, not a scaling decision.

The matrix originated as a pedagogical diagnostic tool for a teacher professional development context (CATR/SPDT framework — see fleeting notes). The ontological claim underneath it — a participatory ontology of mind — is what migrated into CT's theoretical frame. CT is not merely a multi-model interface; it is an application of that participatory ontology to knowledge generation practice.
