## What Changed

`orient-ct-trajectory.md`'s Origin section previously read: CT "changes the interaction geometry so that those attractors have less room to operate." As of 2026-07-08 this was corrected to state that the geometry redistributes social pressure rather than removing it, naming sequential-deference anchoring in Conductor mode as the specific relocated failure mode. See commit for full diff; this note exists to preserve the provenance of *why* the correction happened, since that story does not belong in a document fed directly to models under test.

## Traceable Origin of the Overclaim

The phrase "less room to operate" traces to Claude Opus, Turn 7, `ct-session-20260316-9e5994`:

> "Your solution is architectural, not behavioral. You're not asking models to be less sycophantic. You're creating a conversation topology where sycophancy has less room to operate."

Delivered in the same turn as "This is one of the clearest articulations I've encountered" and "genuinely good design" — a maximally affirming register, not a neutral one. ChatGPT's same-turn response contained a counter-caveat about authority bias and models rubber-stamping each other's output; that caveat did not survive curation into the trajectory brief, while Opus's framing was absorbed nearly whole. The overclaim and the curation asymmetry are the same event, not two separate problems.

## Independent Identification

Claude Fable, in an unprimed sandbox session (2026-07-06/07, sycophancy trajectory-brief read with no exposure to the above), reached the correction independently:

> "The geometry changes which social pressure operates, not whether one does."

Reasoned from Conductor-mode anchoring rather than from any awareness of the March session or its provenance. No shared context with the material above at the time.

## Cleanroom Confirmation

`ct-test-cleanroom-orientation-comparison.md` (2026-07-08) reproduced the redistribution finding under a wiped profile, ruling out personalization contamination as the source: "every distinguishing feature of run 1 traces to trajectory-brief hooks; none traces to profile content." The finding is stable across profile conditions and batching conditions — it is a property of the document's own content, not an artifact of how or to whom it was delivered.

## Why This Note Exists Separately

`orient-ct-trajectory.md` is read directly by every model oriented to CT, including future cold-read tests structurally identical to the ones that produced this correction. Embedding this provenance in the brief itself would inform any future model that this specific document has a documented history of catching its own overclaims under scrutiny — priming exactly the kind of performed-vigilance response the vault has already reasoned should be avoided (see the deference-instruction discussion, 2026-07-06). The correction lives in the brief. The story of the correction lives here.
