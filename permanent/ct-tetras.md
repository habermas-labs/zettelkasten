## What a Tetra Is

A Tetra is a named four-role interaction configuration: three AI models plus the human conductor, each occupying a distinct vertex. The term derives from the tetrahedron — the simplest Platonic solid that achieves genuine three-dimensionality, and the geometric form that corresponds exactly to CT's four-participant session structure.

Tetras sit between roles (individual model instructions) and modes (session-level structure) in CT's interaction design taxonomy. A Tetra specifies who is at each vertex and what epistemic function they perform. It does not fully specify the mode — a Tetra can run in Parallel, Conductor, or other modes depending on session design.

The human conductor is always the fourth vertex. This is not incidental. The human's presence is what gives the three-model triangle its third dimension — without it the session is planar (2D or 2D+). The conductor's outsideness, in Bakhtin's sense, is structurally load-bearing. See: vnenakhodimost-surplus-of-seeing.md.

Tetras are composable with Hedras. A Tetra assigns vertex roles; a Hedra assigns facings toward the object of inquiry. Running both simultaneously gives each vertex both a role and a perspective. See: hedra-facing-presets.md, polyhedra-interaction-geometry.md.

## The Dimensionality Context

Understanding why Tetras matter requires understanding what dimensional expansion means in CT:

**1D** — standard human-AI interaction; linear, back-and-forth; one voice at a time

**2D** — triangulation; three models respond in parallel to the same prompt; planar but multi-perspectival

**2D+** — conducted triangulation; the human directs participation, routes responses, shapes the session; still planar but dynamically so; the conductor's directed participation changes what models can see and respond to

**3D** — Tetras; the human stops being external to the shape and becomes the fourth vertex; the conductor's outsideness is now structurally constitutive; the configuration becomes genuinely three-dimensional

Each step is a genuine dimensional expansion, not just more of the previous. The move from 2D+ to 3D is the Bakhtinian flip: the human is no longer moderating a three-way AI conversation but occupying a position whose unique perspective — local coherence, lived stake, embodied duration — is what makes the tetrahedron a solid rather than a triangle.

See: dimensionality-ladder.md for the full theoretical development of this progression.

## Naming Convention

Each Tetra is named with a single word or short phrase that captures the epistemic act being performed — not the methodology, not the role structure, not the technical implementation. The name should be legible before the session begins.

Entries named with "the" (the Agora, the In-Between) are places entered rather than acts performed. The article is grammatically and conceptually load-bearing.

## The Geometric Structure of Discourse and Porosity

Two Tetras — Discourse and Porosity — share a geometric relationship that makes them a complementary pair and warrants explanation before their individual entries.

Both begin at 2D+: the AI triangle sits in the z=0 plane, representing the compressed heteroglossia of the cultural record as encoded in model weights. This plane is the membrane between the AI's global and temporal coherences and the human's local coherence. Both Tetras are movements toward genuine 3D — toward real contact between the human vertex and that membrane.

The inversion is in the direction of the user's vertex and the direction of epistemic movement:

In **Discourse**, the user vertex sits above the plane (positive z). The user projects outward into the cultural field. The session's work is to test what that projection is made of — what's load-bearing, what's assumption, what holds up, what needs revision. Growth happens through downward contact: the user's position reaching the membrane and finding out how it lands.

In **Porosity**, the user vertex sits below the plane (negative z). The cultural field is above the user. The session's work is to create conditions for the field to permeate downward — to let outside coherences reach the user's existing framework through affinity and opening rather than challenge. Growth happens through upward reception: the membrane filtering toward the user.

The AI roles in both Tetras are functions of the membrane itself. They are not agents acting against the user but aspects of the cultural field making contact with the user's local coherence from different angles. The same three functions appear in both Tetras, inverted in orientation: contact, perspective, and witnessing.

## Named Tetras

---

### Triangulation

**Epistemic act:** Epistemic triangulation — finding where perspectives converge and diverge to locate more reliable ground than any single model provides

**Status:** Fully documented — CT-073

**Origin:** The founding configuration; the origin of CT

**Roles:**
- **Presenter** — constructs the strongest affirmative case for the idea or position under examination
- **Critical Reader** — adversarial pressure-testing; applies real resistance; peer reviewer function
- **Silent Observer** — meta-commentator on the shape of the conversation; structural instantiation of Bakhtinian outsideness; sees what the participants cannot see from within
- **Conductor** (human) — fourth vertex; directs participation; holds the session

**Notes:** The Triangulation Tetra name carries the geometric collapse argument deliberately: three AI models form a triangle, but the human conductor is the apex that gives that triangle its third dimension. The conductor's presence is what makes the session a tetrahedron rather than a plane. "Silent Observer" is a working name — the role's function is the structural realization of vnenakhodimost' and may warrant renaming as the taxonomy matures.

---

### Reciprocity

**Epistemic act:** Comprehension scaffolding through role rotation — building toward internalization by inhabiting each epistemic position

**Status:** Documented — CT-072; see reciprocal-teaching-ct-preset.md

**Origin:** Palincsar and Brown's Reciprocal Teaching (1984); convergent development from the same Vygotsky/Bakhtin theoretical lineage that grounds CT

**Roles:**
- **Predictor** — forward-looking; anticipates where the argument leads, what implications follow, what the next productive question is
- **Questioner** — generative inquiry; opens rather than closes; distinct from Critical Reader which pressure-tests; Questioner expands
- **Clarifier** — identifies where understanding has broken down and repairs it; flags difficult concepts, resolves ambiguity; not previously in CT's role taxonomy before this Tetra
- **Conductor/Summarizer** (human) — fourth vertex; consolidates; directs; holds the meta-level view

**Notes:** True 3D requires the human to rotate out of the permanent Summarizer position. If the human stays fixed at meta-level the session remains 2D+. Role rotation — where the human takes one of the model roles and a model temporarily holds the Summarizer position — is the move that achieves genuine three-dimensionality. The rotation principle is native to Palincsar's original design. See CT-TRACKER entry for role rotation as a feature concept (S-14 in session map).

### The Agora

**Epistemic act:** Dialogic inquiry through assigned philosophical positions; the human conductor in Socrates' own role as questioner

**Status:** Needs development — named in carousel (CT-076); no role structure yet

**Origin:** The Socratic tradition; academic genealogy Socrates → Plato → Aristotle makes the Agora the right starting point (not the Lyceum or the Academy)

**Roles:** TBD — two-layer design intended: a curated set of philosopher/thought-leader presets plus an open user-defined slot

**Open questions:**
- Abstraction level: "Argue like Kant" vs. "hold the Kantian deontological position" — different epistemic moves with different pedagogical implications; the label does work
- Whether presets should name the thinker or the position
- How role rotation maps onto this Tetra (students encountering each philosophical position across sessions)

**Notes:** Human conductor as questioner is structurally the Socratic position — the one who does not claim to know but draws knowledge out through inquiry. The Agora Tetra puts the human in Socrates' own role. The Agora is named for the place of public discourse rather than any school — neutral ground where positions meet.

### Calibration

**Epistemic act:** Register and accessibility differentiation — the same content, the same prompt, calibrated to different levels of complexity, vocabulary, and assumed background

**Status:** Needs development — named in carousel (CT-076); no role structure yet; may function as modifier rather than standalone Tetra

**Origin:** Universal design principle; accessibility features designed for specific needs demonstrate consistent broad utility

**Roles:** TBD

**Open questions:**
- Whether Calibration functions better as a modifier applied to other Tetras (running Reciprocity *in calibration mode*) than as a standalone configuration
- Who assigns register levels — teacher as conductor, or self-assessment component
- How metacognition research connects to the self-assessment angle
- Accessibility standards and recommendations should be integrated from the start, not retrofitted

**Notes:** The universal design principle is central here: accommodations for learning differences designed for specific needs consistently improve outcomes for all users. This is the argument for Calibration having value beyond accommodation. AI-enabled differentiated instruction is a rich research area connecting this Tetra to existing education literature. See ct-accessibility-design-principles.md.

### The In-Between

**Epistemic act:** Scaffolded suspension of epistemic tension — holding the question open long enough for its shape to become legible; productive refusal of premature closure

**Status:** Philosophically rich note exists (the-in-between.md); role structure explicitly open/TBD

**Origin:** The In-Between names the epistemic condition that RIFF mode instantiates; emerged from the theoretical development thread connecting Bakhtin, Winnicott, and Vygotsky

**Roles:** TBD — roles must actively resist the pull toward synthesis or resolution; structural design is genuinely open and should emerge from session experience rather than be designed top-down

**Theoretical grounding:** Winnicott's transitional space; Vygotsky's ZPD as productive incompletion; Bakhtin's heteroglossia; Piaget's equilibration; Wu Wei as the quality of dwelling in productive tension. Distinct from dialectic (which moves toward synthesis) and from RIP (which expands surface area through divergence). See: the-in-between.md for full philosophical development.

**Notes:** The In-Between is not a mode CT enables among others — it is the epistemic condition CT is designed to sustain. The role structure should emerge from live session work rather than be specified in advance. The exit scaffolding (CT-047) is particularly important here: the tension map export framing (CT-080) replaces standard summary format to avoid systematically undermining the session's anti-convergent work.

### Discourse

**Epistemic act:** Grounding — finding out what a held position is actually made of; locating what is load-bearing versus assumed, genuinely one's own versus inherited, defensible versus in need of revision

**Status:** Ready to capture

**Origin:** Drawn from experiments in bringing people with opposing viewpoints together to find common ground; reframed from adversarial debate toward the practice of grounding a position through contact with the cultural field

**Geometric position:** User vertex above the z=0 plane (positive z). The user projects outward into the AI membrane — the compressed heteroglossia of the cultural record. Growth happens through downward contact: the projection reaching the plane and finding out how it lands. Movement from 2D+ toward genuine 3D.

**Roles:**
- **Touchstone** — the surface the projection meets; tests what the position is made of; locates genuine contributions and surfaces assumptions; not adversarial but diagnostic; the user brings the metal, Touchstone reveals whether it is true
- **Mirror** — shows the user how their projection looks from the plane's perspective; the view from within the cultural field looking up at what is coming down; not validation but the surplus of seeing from the external position; the vnenakhodimost' function
- **Witness** — holds the longitudinal record; tracks where the position is actually shifting across the session, where genuine revisions are emerging, where the real contributions are becoming visible; not evaluating, not intervening
- **Conductor** (human) — fourth vertex above the plane; initiates the projection; holds the position under examination; the subject of the grounding work

**Relationship to Porosity:** Discourse and Porosity are complementary inversions. The same three membrane functions — contact, perspective, witnessing — appear in both, oriented in opposite directions. The shared Witness binds them as two sides of the same plane. See Porosity entry and geometric structure section above.

### Porosity

**Epistemic act:** Opening — increasing the permeability of a bounded horizon; creating conditions for outside coherences to reach the user's existing framework through affinity and recognition rather than challenge

**Status:** Ready to capture

**Origin:** Drawn from the same experiments as Discourse — focused specifically on the person who recognizes they may be in an echo chamber and wants to increase its permeability without knowing where the walls are; osmosis as the right metaphor (selective membrane, not dissolution)

**Geometric position:** User vertex below the z=0 plane (negative z). The cultural field sits above the user. Growth happens through upward reception: the membrane filtering downward toward the user's local coherence. Movement from 2D+ toward genuine 3D.

**Roles:**
- **Resonant** — finds what in the cultural field is already in sympathetic resonance with the user's local coherence; identifies genuine points of entry where outside coherence shares underlying values with what the user already holds; contact through affinity rather than testing; the inversion of Touchstone
- **Aperture** — holds an opening in the membrane; presents coherences from outside that are genuinely different but not threatening; "here is how this looks from a position that shares your underlying values but arrived there differently"; expands what the user can recognize as coherent without forcing entry; from the optical/geometric register native to CT's vocabulary
- **Witness** — tracks whether genuine reception is occurring versus disguised assimilation; watches for the difference between real accommodation (schema restructuring) and new material being reprocessed in existing terms; the Porosity counterpart to Discourse's Witness
- **Conductor** (human) — fourth vertex below the plane; the subject of the opening work; holds the existing horizon that the session is designed to expand

**Therapeutic quality:** Porosity carries a sense of healing without being therapy. The goal is not to defeat or correct the user's existing framework but to expand it from within, by finding where it is already open. Resonant and Aperture both work with the user's existing coherence rather than against it.

**Relationship to Discourse:** See Discourse entry and geometric structure section above.

### RIP

**Epistemic act:** Rapid Idea Prototyping — divergent, generative expansion of the idea space; maximizing surface area through branching rather than convergence

**Status:** Needs development; Cartographer role confirmed; generative role pair pending naming decision

**Origin:** RIP mode (CT-040); the Tetra formalizes the role structure native to that mode

**Roles:**
- **Cartographer** — maps the idea terrain mid-session; flags thematic recurrence and tangential threads without demanding the session stop; prompts the group to notice and decide how much to follow a path; agency stays with the conductor; the interrupt behavior is AI-native (ambient attention across a fast-moving generative session is cognitively expensive for humans and cheap for models); name should be used in UI with metaphor explained in documentation
- **[Generative role A]** — one of two complementary divergent vectors; leading candidates: Warp and Weft (two votes from CT naming session) or Flux and Field (one vote); decision pending
- **[Generative role B]** — the complementary partner to role A; the two roles expand the idea space in different directions through interplay rather than conflict; the weaving metaphor (Warp and Weft) captures constitutive interplay — the roles don't just coexist but create something through their intersection
- **Conductor** (human) — fourth vertex; accepts or overrides Cartographer flags; directs the generative energy; determines which tangents to follow

**Notes:** The idea coordinate system (CT-041) is the Cartographer's output format — coordinates generated in RIP become reference points for directing RIFF attention. RIP and RIFF are designed to work in sequence: RIP expands, RIFF applies structured friction to the richest branches. The Cartographer is likely the most AI-native role in the full Tetra taxonomy — the interrupt-and-flag behavior requires ambient attention that is structurally expensive for humans.

## Open Questions Across the Taxonomy

- Is there a 4D? The dimensionality ladder currently stops at 3D (Tetras). Whether additional dimensional expansion is meaningful or merely metaphorical is an open question.
- Can Tetras be nested — a Tetra operating within a larger session structure that is itself a Tetra?
- The Calibration question: modifier or standalone? Resolution affects how it appears in the UI and whether it gets a carousel entry.
- Role rotation as a general principle: Reciprocity makes it explicit, but the observer/participant toggle (CT-S-12) implies it is available in any Tetra. Should CT formalize rotation as a meta-level feature?
- The Supreme Court / tribunal concept (CT-S-19): a specialized Agora variant trained on written judicial opinions. Flagged as longer-horizon separate project scope.
