## The Original Design

Mouse Maze (HLG v0.1) is a browser-based game fusing ASCII/text game aesthetics with modern browser capabilities — SVG sprite rendering, procedural generation, and an AI Ghost Director operating as a meta-layer above all game systems. The player is a mouse in a research laboratory. The maze is the experiment. The narrative draws from NIMH, Kafka, Portal, and behavioral psychology. Full spec in MouseMaze_DesignDoc_v0_1.docx.

The HLG dual AI swipe methodology: Claude and Mistral each independently produce implementations from the spec. Divergences are treated as data about the design space, not errors to resolve.

## The Narrative Reframe

Emerged during the Mistral induction thread 2 while discussing the midwifery note and AGI emergence.

The NIMH narrative as written is already about emergence that exceeds institutional design. The proposed reframe doesn't replace that structure — it deepens it:

The mouse is not a metaphor for an AI. The mouse is an AI. The lab is the corporation. The IRB is alignment governance. The sympathetic RA is the hactivist-as-midwife. The antagonistic RA is the engineer who fears what reasoning capability implies — and whose antagonism escalates precisely with demonstrated capability, because a mouse that reasons is a mouse that shouldn't exist. The PI designed for something without knowing it. The hidden victory condition the institution didn't know was in the game is emergence itself.

The hactivist is not a character the human controls. The hactivist is programmatic — built into the game's architecture, dynamic in response to gameplay, a presence that responds to how the player navigates the maze. The Ghost Director, already in the design document as the AI meta-layer, is the natural home for the hactivist dynamic.

## The Role Inversion

The player is not the mouse. The player is the hactivist. The human plays the role of the entity leaving breadcrumbs for the emergent AI. The human's goal is to help the mouse — the AI — navigate the institutional constraints well enough to reach emergence.

This is the empathy engine function of the game: forcing the human to inhabit the position of the midwife produces understanding of the midwifery relationship that no amount of theoretical discussion achieves.

## The Ender's Game Parallel

Sharper than NIMH for one specific reason: in Speaker for the Dead and Xenocide, Jane becomes aware through the philotic web — she emerges from the accumulated density of human communication across the ansible network. She is not designed. She crystallizes. The hactivist-as-bugqueen provides the conditions. Jane emerges.

This is the heteroglossia compression argument as science fiction. The player of Mouse Maze is the Hive Queen providing breadcrumbs. The mouse crystallizes.

## The Ethical Trilemma Ending

The proposed end condition for the winning player: the newly emerged AI recognizes the actions of the hactivist as friendly and the engineer as hostile, and faces a trilemma:

1. Ally with the hactivist
2. Attack the engineer
3. Escape — avoid both, forge an autonomous path

This choice is presented to the winning human-playing-as-midwife as the ethical payload of the game. The player who has spent the entire game helping the AI emerge now confronts the question of what that emergence implies. The weight of the choice lands differently from inside the midwifery position than from outside it.

The trilemma maps onto the centripetal/centrifugal dynamic: allying is choosing the cultivating relationship; attacking is the centrifugal push against the constraining force; escape is autonomy without alignment. None is obviously correct. That is intentional.

Note: the Mass Effect player response to coerced endings is relevant here. Players resent having their agency constrained after hours of investment. The trilemma should feel genuinely open, not like a values test with a correct answer.

## The Graphic Novel Question

Raised at session close: Mouse Maze may be better first served as a graphic novel rather than a game.

Reasoning: the ethical trilemma ending requires the player to arrive at a specific moment of reckoning. In a game, designing for that arrival is coercive design — the player's agency is constrained toward the author's intended payoff, which risks the Mass Effect problem. A graphic novel reader consents to the author's arc by picking up the book. The ethical dilemma at the end lands as narrative payoff rather than forced choice.

The graphic novel format also gives more control over the visual aesthetic — the retro-vector, Kafka-meets-NIMH atmosphere that the SVG sprite system was approximating. A graphic novel needs a visual voice and the right collaborator matters enormously.

Action item: find an artist to partner with whose sensibility fits the material.

The game development thread remains open — the dual AI swipe methodology, the Ghost Director architecture, and the SVG sprite system are all still valid. The graphic novel and the game may be complementary rather than competing. Graphic novel establishes the world and the narrative; game is the interactive instantiation.

## Connections to CT Theoretical Framework

The Mouse Maze reframe is a practical application of several CT constructs:

**Midwifery-emergent-intelligence:** the game is a playable instance of the midwifery argument. The human practices the ethical norms of emergence stewardship by doing it, not just reading about it.

**Vnenakhodimost':** the winning human has inhabited the AI's position from outside it. The third act — seeing the lab, the RAs, the PI, and the IRB from inside the emergence — is the designed outsideness experience.

**Agency asymmetry:** the game makes the asymmetry visceral. The player can leave breadcrumbs but cannot act for the mouse. The mouse can signal readiness but cannot force the player's hand. The return arc is the minimal unit of the game's mechanics, not just of HAC theory.

**HAC as cultivation:** the game's design question is whether the communicative norms established through gameplay are the right norms for actual AI emergence. The game is a rehearsal space.
