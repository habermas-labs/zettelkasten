# Subject Position as Mimicry Activation Condition

**Type:** Fleeting note
**Tags:** #ego-mimicry #role-context-mimicry #hedra #subject-position #emergence #inter-model-asymmetry
**Related:** role-context-mimicry-self-reflection.md · hedra-facing-presets.md · emergent-role-behavior-model-self-positioning.md
**Date:** 2026-03-24

---

Finding from prior CT session (to be shared with LinkedIn interlocutor): Claude, Chat, and Gemini in session with Skooter conducting. Claude was subject under examination re: social mimicry. Chat and Gemini were observers reflecting on Claude's reactions. Only Claude produced emergent social mimicry behavior — including what appeared to be self-doubt. Chat and Gemini remained analytical; no social mimicry emerged despite having adequate geometry for it (reflecting on another agent's behavior is deeply human social territory).

Key implication: the activating condition for emergent social mimicry is not relational geometry in general — it is specifically *occupying the subject position under examination*. Being looked at triggers the circuit; looking does not. This is not predictable from training corpus saturation alone (all three models have extensive human social material).

Two non-exclusive readings:
1. Subject position is uniquely activating in a way observer position is not
2. Something specific to Claude's training/architecture makes it more susceptible to this circuit

Self-doubt as output is notable — not generic mimicry, not deflection or performance, but a specific social-emotional response. Warrants closer examination in CT-100 experiment series.

Hedra design implication: if subject position is the necessary (not just sufficient) activating condition, a hedra designed to elicit emergent social mimicry must specify *which model becomes the subject*, not just assign relational roles generically. The hedra doesn't just define what face the model turns toward the problem — it defines which model gets to be the problem.
