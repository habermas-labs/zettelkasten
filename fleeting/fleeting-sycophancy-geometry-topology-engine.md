## Two formulations from session 20260316-9e5994

### 1. Sycophancy as geometric property of 1D conversation

Emerged from Claude Opus, Turn 7, ct-session-20260316-final.md:

> "Sycophancy isn't just a model flaw to be patched. It's a *geometric property* of 1D conversation. When there's only one model and one user, the gravitational pull toward agreement is structural. No amount of 'please be honest with me' prompting fully escapes it."

And the paired observation:

> "Your solution is architectural, not behavioral. You're not asking models to be less sycophantic. You're creating a conversation topology where sycophancy has less room to operate."

This is a precise and original formulation. The implication: CT's parallel default is not a feature that counteracts sycophancy — it is a structural reconfiguration that makes the sycophantic attractor weaker by design. The fix is in the geometry, not the prompting.

### 2. Conversation topology engine

Emerged from Claude Opus, Turn 6, same session, in response to description of Tetras and interface geometries:

> "What strikes me is that you're not just building a multi-model chat interface. You're building a **conversation topology engine.** The tetras are presets for different *shapes of thinking.*"

"Topology" is doing real work — topology is the study of properties preserved under continuous deformation. A Tetra is a topological object: the epistemic shape of the session can change while the structural relationships between vertices remain intact. Not just a metaphor.

Appropriate audience: AI/tech-literate audiences, researchers, demo context with informed interlocutors. Pairs with the plain-language version ("three models, one conductor, richer thinking") for general audiences.
