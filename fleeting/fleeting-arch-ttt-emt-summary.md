## BIG IDEA 1: Cognitive onboarding

**Origin:** Skooter introduces the term unprompted as the reciprocal of cognitive offloading.

**Core distinction:**
- Cognitive offloading = pushing cognitive work outward into tools (CO literature, EMT)
- Cognitive onboarding = the world pushing cognitive patterns back inward; the inward flow that offloading literature has no canonical name for

**Chat's framing (confirmed by Skooter):** "Offloading changes how we think right now. Onboarding changes who we become over time."

**Ethical stakes:** If onboarding happens automatically through repeated AI use, then AI design is a cognitive development environment whether intended as one or not. A ZPD-aligned AI must manage both flows deliberately: healthy offloading reduces unnecessary cognitive friction; healthy onboarding internalizes reasoning capacity rather than reasoning shortcuts.

**Unhealthy onboarding examples:** absorbing AI errors or biases, over-internalizing shallow shortcuts, losing the ability to reason without external aids, collapsing ZPD boundaries, uncritical acceptance of AI framing.

**Hard problem connection:** If phenomenology depends partly on internalized cultural scaffolds, then cognitive onboarding shapes phenomenological horizons — not metaphorically but through the neuroplasticity loop (see BIG IDEA 2 below).

**ZK candidate:** Standalone permanent note. Connects to workload-outsourcing.md (extends the frame from offloading to bidirectional flow) and to ethics-of-ai-engagement-practice.md (ethical stakes of AI as cognitive development environment).

## BIG IDEA 2: Vygotsky and the hard/easy problem reframe

**Origin:** Skooter observes that Western cognitive science has domesticated Vygotsky into a functional scaffolding theorist, stripping out the ontological claim.

**The move:** "Mind in Society" is not a metaphor for "learning benefits from social context." It is a metaphysical claim: mind is constitutively relational, cultural, historically embedded. This means mind is not located in the individual brain as a container — it exists in the between-space of social activity, language, tools, and practices.

**Consequence for Chalmers:** The hard problem is formulated assuming the locus of phenomenal experience is the isolated individual brain. If Vygotsky is taken seriously at the ontological level, the unit of analysis is wrong. The question "why does this internal functional process produce subjective experience?" becomes malformed — because "internal" presupposes a boundary that Vygotsky denies.

**The neuroplasticity loop (Skooter's addition):** Culture doesn't only scaffold cognition; through experience-dependent neuroplasticity it literally restructures the neural substrate that generates phenomenology. Therefore:
- culture → shapes → neural architecture → shapes → form of experience → shapes → participation in culture → (loop)
- AI use over time onboards reasoning patterns into neural substrate via same mechanism as literacy, music training, language acquisition — just faster and at scale

**Synthesis:** The hard problem isn't dissolved, but its clean internalism is undermined. Phenomenology may still arise internally, but its form is co-shaped by the social-technological field. "Experience isn't a property of isolated brains; it is a property of culturally shaped, environmentally embedded organisms." (Chat's formulation, confirmed as accurate by Skooter.)

**Chat's convergence point:** Vygotsky (mind as social) + Bakhtin (consciousness as dialogue) + Heidegger (being as situated) + Clark/Chalmers (extended mind) + enactivism + ZPD-AI + cognitive exoskeleton + hermeneutic circle analogy "all coheres." One question emerges: if cognitive work is distributed but conscious experience is not — what does it mean to own a thought?

**ZK candidate:** Permanent note connecting sociocultural-hermeneutics.md, ethics-of-becoming.md, and the local-coherence-ai-instantiation.md frame.

## BIG IDEA 3: 3×3 participation mode matrix — origin session

**Confirmed:** This thread contains the founding session for the 3×3 participation mode matrix. Previously sought; now located.

**Trigger:** Conversation about distributed/relational mind leads Skooter to observe that this perspective "gets at that good use/bad use or garbage/gems idea." The matrix is conceived as a normative structure for evaluating AI-human cognition in education.

**Genesis of the 3×3:**
- Initial intuition: 2×2 (human × AI, passive vs. active)
- Rejection of "distributed mind" as a label for the underlying theory — implies a non-distributed mind, which Skooter doesn't hold with
- Move to 3×3 via Bakhtin: purely active participation without feedback = univocal voice; purely receptive = passive; integration of receptiveness and expressiveness = generative, aligned with Bakhtin's multivocal voice
- Skooter names his philosophical bias explicitly and doesn't care: "generative" is normatively marked from the start

**Chat's characterization of what Skooter is doing:** "Inventing a participatory ontology of mind." This framing captures what migrated from TTT into CT's theoretical core — CT is not just a multi-model interface but an application of a participatory ontology of mind to knowledge generation practice.

**Full 9-cell development:** Appears later in same transcript. All nine cells developed with labels and examples; all confirmed non-dross by end of session. Cell 9 (Generative × Generative) = "Dialogic Mind-in-Participation" — the Bakhtinian sweet spot, the cognitive exoskeleton sweet spot, the CT ideal.

**Note:** The matrix originated as a pedagogical diagnostic tool for TTT. The ontological claim underneath it (participatory ontology of mind) is what migrated into CT's theoretical frame.
