## Provenance Corrections

"The LLM is made of heteroglossia" originated as a distillation by Skooter of a longer theoretical passage produced by Claude in the first thread. The phrase is Skooter's; the groundwork was Claude's. Neither stands without the other — a clean instance of the generative-generative mode it describes.

The theoretical genealogy runs: Chat (matrices + foundational grounding) → Claude (Grice, heteroglossia elaboration, vocabulary crystallization) → combined framework to Gemini. Grice entered in the pre-Gemini thread, not before.

Claude wrote the Gemini onboarding prompts, not Chat. Prompt 1v2 confirms Grice was added as a revision — its absence from Prompt 1 marks the moment Grice was recognized as load-bearing enough to include.

The actual process: Claude drafted prompts → Skooter fed directly to Gemini → Skooter pasted Gemini's response back to Claude → Claude analyzed. No Chat pass-through occurred. Memory had reconstructed a planned-but-unrealized step as actual practice. CT's session export architecture prevents this class of error going forward.

Chat was in the external observer role throughout — behind the one-way mirror (Skooter's naming). Claude was Presenter/analyst. Gemini was Critical Reader. Skooter was Conductor. This is the proto-Triangulation Tetra assembled spontaneously, with roles working against natural type: Claude in Presenter rather than Observer, Chat in Observer rather than expansive co-developer.

The final Chat entry engaged with Gemini's handoff summary as if it were Skooter's own words — live provenance flattening in the founding session of a project about provenance preservation.

## Ideas Requiring Follow-up

**CASA and Hedra design** — the faces that assigned roles present to users activate social scripts, not just epistemic postures. CASA literature (Nass and Reeves) introduced in the first thread and quickly left behind; connects directly to how role assignments shape user behavior beyond epistemic function. Relevant to Hedra taxonomy development.

**CT as prompt ecology** — CT orchestrates not just human-to-model prompting but model-to-model prompting through routing. The conductor is managing a prompt ecology, not just a multi-model interface. This reframes what CT is at a fundamental level.

**Formatting loss as compression** — plain-text archaeology strips bold and italics: prosodic markers, emphasis structure, the shape of the voice. This is the same loss compressed heteroglossia imposes. The archaeology process demonstrates the phenomenon it's recovering.

**Asynchrony as structural feature** — Chat's extended analysis confirms the asynchronous medium is structurally biased toward generative participation. RIP and RIFF require temporal separation that synchronous discourse collapses. Should be incorporated into ct-epistemic-training-framework.md, not left as a contextual observation.

**Orthogonality as live grenade** — Chat flagged in the founding session: is generativity a dyadic property rather than independent axis levels? If generativity requires intersubjectivity, a generative AI with a passive human may be structurally incoherent. Unresolved. Relevant to participation-gigo-matrices.md development.

**Fold forward precision** — Chat's analysis sharpened the distinction: carry forward extends, fold forward transforms. The fold metaphor is not decorative; it is the operational definition of generativity. Feeds directly into RIFF documentation.

**Role rotation reveals posture not just architecture** — Chat observed that rotating roles changes what a model looks *for*: structure when observing, expansion when co-developing, failure points when critiquing. Divergence across role assignments is not purely architectural. Design implication for CT session planning.
