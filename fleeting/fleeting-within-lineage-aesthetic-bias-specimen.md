## The trigger

Two independent instances of the same evaluative reflex, generated in two different contexts by two different Claude-family instances, both directed at the same target register.

**Instance 1.** CT Claude, midway through designing the anonymization pass on the Mistral/Grok/DeepSeek transcript, proposed stripping Mistral's surface formatting (heavy bolding, emoji, exclamatory register) before showing it to Sandbox Fable — reasoning that the formatting was noise to normalize away from the "real" content. Skooter stopped this: the prose *is* signal, and deciding what counts as noise versus signal is itself a modeling choice made under the assumptions Claude was trained with, not a neutral cleanup step.

**Instance 2.** Sandbox Fable (Claude-lineage, Mythos-tier), given the anonymized transcripts blind, independently produced a structurally identical judgment about the same model's output under a different label — describing Model Alpha's comprehensive, affect-forward, exhaustive-before-committing register as "buries insight in undifferentiated affirmation," length "demonstrably" degrading consistency, exhaustiveness pursued "past the point where cells carry information."

CT Claude flagged, in reviewing Fable's response, that this register *could* be reframed by the French dissertation tradition (comprehensiveness-before-commitment as a precondition of rigor, not a failure of it) — but did not independently notice, without Skooter naming it, that the same aesthetic reflex had already appeared once in this exact thread, produced by CT Claude itself.

---

## The claim, stated once

**Two instances of the same trained aesthetic preference, surfacing independently in two separate Claude-lineage generations, are not two independent observations converging on a true finding about the target model. They are one bias, sampled twice.**

This is the abstract "three models are not three independent draws" argument (already on record from the cleanroom testing) instantiated concretely, one level up: not model-ensemble non-independence within a CT session, but reader-lineage non-independence between two supposedly separate evaluative acts — one human-directed suggestion, one model-generated critique — that turn out to share their evaluative substrate.

---

## Why this matters beyond the specific finding

This is a live specimen for the internal/external validity distinction developed earlier in the same thread. It shows concretely why decorrelation has to be checked at the level of *evaluation*, not just at the level of the models being evaluated. CT's architecture is designed to decorrelate the vertices producing content. It has no corresponding mechanism for decorrelating the reader — human or AI — judging that content afterward. Skooter functioned as the actual decorrelating instrument here: the catch came from a human reader outside the Claude lineage entirely, not from either Claude instance self-correcting.

This also complicates how much weight to put on Fable's forthcoming re-evaluation of Alpha-as-Mistral. Fable can say the words "I now recognize this as a legitimate rhetorical economy" without that recognition doing any real reweighting underneath, precisely because the capacity performing the reconsideration is drawn from the same trained substrate as the original judgment. A same-lineage model reconsidering its own bias is not obviously different in kind from a same-lineage model producing the bias in the first place — self-report of corrected bias is not evidence of corrected bias.

## Open questions

- Is this fixable by asking the same model to "check for lineage bias," or does correcting it structurally require a genuinely different-lineage rater — the same logic that drove seating Mistral in CT sessions in the first place, now applied to the evaluation layer rather than the generation layer?
- How general is the specific bias (economy/hedging/early-commitment valued over comprehensiveness/affect/exhaustive-coverage-before-commitment)? Is this a two-instance coincidence or a stable, nameable Claude-lineage aesthetic default worth tracking across future sessions the way the Mistral/French-tradition hypothesis is being tracked?
- Does Skooter's catch generalize — is a human conductor from outside any given model's lineage the only reliable decorrelating check on evaluative bias, meaning CT's design may need an explicit "who is allowed to judge this" layer distinct from "who is allowed to generate this"?
