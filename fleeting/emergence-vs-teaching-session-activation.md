# Emergence vs. Teaching — Session Activation

Cluster of related ideas from session — separating three questions that are distinct:

**1. Emergence vs. teaching:** Social mimicry session suggests human presence isn't providing content — it's providing *activating conditions* for latent behavior. Can't train a model to have emergent behavior directly; can train it on enough human social material that the behavior becomes available, then session geometry determines activation. Human in session completes a circuit, not adds to training.

**2. Minimum training threshold:** What is the minimum training required for social mimicry to start emerging in other areas? Probably unanswerable from outside — asks about threshold conditions for emergence, which aren't observable. More tractable question: what session geometries reliably activate vs. suppress the emergence? That's observable and designable.

**3. Hedra preloading:** Can this kind of training/emergence be preloaded into a hedra? Key insight: you don't preload the *behavior* — you preload the *conditions*. A hedra that assigns a model the subject-under-examination role creates the geometry that completes the circuit. Role assignment → geometry → circuit completion. This is consistent with hedra-as-epistemic-geometry framing: not telling models what to do, but defining what face they turn toward the problem (or, in this case, which model becomes the problem).
