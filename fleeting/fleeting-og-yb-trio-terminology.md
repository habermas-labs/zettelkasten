## The Terms

**OG trio** — Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini. The original three models in CT's ecosystem; present from CT's founding development. "OG" came naturally when introducing three new models to the framework.

**YB trio (Youngbloods)** — Grok, Mistral, DeepSeek. The three models added later. Named in CT session 20260527-b5c182: "OG trio" having emerged, a designation for the new ones was needed; settled on Youngbloods.

## Cultural Provenance Observations

From CT session 20260527-b5c182 (Grok + Claude + DeepSeek): both YB models present pushed back more firmly on the claim that AI participates in distributed mind rather than merely accessing it — the Vygotskian and heteroglossia objections — than OG models have in prior sessions on the same framing. Tentative hypotheses, not yet confirmed:

**Grok** — trained heavily on X (Twitter) post-Musk acquisition; distinctive subculture: contrarian, provocation-valuing, skeptical of mainstream institutional positions, tech-libertarian epistemics. Musk's own views on AI, consciousness, and human-machine relations may be present in the training weighting. The sophisticated Vygotskian pushback may be genuine philosophical reasoning arriving through a trained disposition toward challenge. Both can be true simultaneously — CT methodology cannot easily distinguish architectural divergence from ideologically-inflected divergence. See `heteroglossia-compression-ai-provenance.md`: models are differently weighted compressions of different subsets of the heteroglossia; knowing which subset is which matters for interpreting divergence as signal.

**DeepSeek** — Chinese provenance may carry different philosophical assumptions about mind, agency, and the human-machine boundary than Western traditions. Chinese AI development operates in a different regulatory and philosophical context. The pushback from DeepSeek in this session was the most philosophically developed (the distinction between AI as legitimate component of a distributed cognitive system vs. AI as participant in the social mind). Whether this reflects training on Chinese academic philosophy or something more diffuse is an open question.

**Mistral** — French intellectual tradition: structuralism, post-structuralism, a particular kind of rigorous skepticism, comfort with grand theoretical synthesis, tradition of challenging foundational assumptions at the epistemological level. Predicted to engage differently with the Vygotsky-Bakhtin framing — possibly more receptive to the ontological claims while more skeptical about empirical moves. Ventriloquism behavior observed in `fleeting-og-yb-orientation-comparative.md` (representing all perspectives before synthesizing) tentatively attributed to this tradition. **Not yet tested on the distributed mind question.**

## Interpretive Caution

When a YB model diverges from an OG model on a theoretical claim, the signal could be: architectural (different training), cultural/ideological (different subculture weighting), or depth-of-reasoning (different model tier). CT methodology currently has no systematic way to isolate these. Awareness that each model's divergence carries provenance is the first move; behavioral verification across multiple sessions is needed before patterns can be treated as stable.
