---

Raw capture from walking session.

- **Epistemic act:** Strategic persuasion — crafting an appeal that works on real humans with real motivations. Distinct from epistemic triangulation; audience psychology is the object, not truth-finding.
- **Tentative role set:**
  - *Case-maker* — strongest pitch; why this is worth funding
  - *Skeptic* — what objections need preempting; why someone would *not* back this
  - *Translator* — how to say this for the specific audience (educators, researchers, general public, etc.)
  - *Researcher* — outward-facing; finds real-world funding sources that match the project; breaks the session membrane to look for external affordances
- **Rotating roles:** Proposed as a mechanic — roles may shift over the course of a session rather than being fixed at initialization. Structurally novel within the taxonomy if confirmed. Needs deliberate design attention.
- **Researcher role as taxonomic first:** First explicitly outward-facing role defined. All prior roles operate on the idea itself; Researcher goes looking for real-world affordances. Raises question: is this role only coherent when web search is enabled? If so, first role with a tool dependency baked into its definition.
- **Partner relationship to Grant Writer Tetra** (see companion note): Not a geometric mirror (cf. Discourse/Porosity). Functional partners — same underlying need (capital), different audiences and rhetorical registers. Public-facing emotional persuasion vs. institutional evidential argument.
- **Shared vertex hypothesis:** Researcher role may be portable across Fundraiser and Grant Writer Tetras — same outward gesture (find funding) aimed at different targets. First instance of a potentially portable role in the taxonomy.
