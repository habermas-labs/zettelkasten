## The trigger

Skooter noticed "residue" surfacing repeatedly across recent notes, unprompted, in contexts that weren't deliberately echoing each other. Worth checking whether it's a coincidence of word choice or a single structural claim showing up at different scales.

## The claim, stated once

**The artifact left behind after a generative process is not identical to the process, and cannot substitute for it, but its existence and shape are evidence that the process occurred.**

Residue is not the process. Residue is not proof of a *good* process. But the absence of residue where residue is expected is diagnostic — it signals no process occurred at all.

## Three (four) instances found so far

**Scale 1 — individual learning episode.** fleeting-irony-of-agency.md, via the Dewey flip: output isn't the proxy for learning; output produced *through* the learning process is the residue of learning, not its evidence independent of that process. Workload outsourcing in its pure form produces output with no residue-generating process behind it.

**Scale 2 — accumulated practice over a career.** fleeting-workspace-friction-development.md, direct quote from the note itself: "the master's idiosyncratic arrangement is sedimented cognitive history... precomputed residue of practice." The apprentice inhabits that residue until it becomes legible — apprenticeship as living inside someone else's accumulated trace.

**Scale 3 — a civilization's worth of writing.** heteroglossia-compression-ai-provenance.md, implicit rather than named: a model's output is framed as carrying the trace of the corpus that trained it — genuine provenance running back through the human thought that produced the training distribution. Not currently using the word "residue," but structurally the same claim: what the model produces is evidence of what it was trained on, without being identical to or a substitute for that underlying human thought.

**Scale 0 — the single move, underneath all three.** kirsh-maglio-epistemic-actions.md's subtraction test is implicitly a residue test: remove the AI's output and ask what's left for the human to still do. "Nothing left to fit" = no residue, pragmatic replacement. "Something left to integrate" = residue exists, epistemic scaffolding occurred. The subtraction test may be best understood as an instrument for detecting residue at the smallest scale, with the other three notes applying the same detection logic at successively larger scales (episode, career, civilization).

## Why this might be worth naming formally

Workload outsourcing was coined rather than borrowed because the existing term (cognitive offloading) misdescribed what was actually happening. The same test applies here: does an existing term already do this work? "Trace," "residue," "sediment," and "evidence" are all doing overlapping labor across these notes without a settled choice among them. If the concept is genuinely operating at multiple scales — which the four instances above suggest — it may deserve the same treatment workload outsourcing got: a term node stating the claim once, with the existing notes updated to point to it rather than each re-deriving it locally.

## Open questions

- Is "residue" the right word, or does it carry unwanted connotation (residue as leftover, waste) that "trace" or "sediment" avoids? Sediment has the advantage of already being used in the workspace-friction note and pairs well with Hutchins' distributed-cognition framing (residue *settles*, accumulates in layers, the way practice does).
- Does this deserve a term node (permanent, definitional) or does it stay a cross-cutting observation living in this fleeting note with links out? Workload outsourcing needed a term node because it was doing argumentative work of its own. Residue may be more of a *pattern* that other terms rely on rather than a term with its own independent claims — worth deciding before promoting.
- Is there a fifth instance hiding in the vault already, unnoticed, the way heteroglossia-compression's use was implicit rather than named? Worth a targeted search during the Fable audit specifically for this pattern.
- Relationship to the unnamed In-Between rotation concept (flagged in fleeting-workspace-friction-development.md): rotating an idea without placing it doesn't leave residue in the Kirsh sense until something is fixed in place. Is rotation the pre-residue state — action without yet a trace?
