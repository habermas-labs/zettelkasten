## Names

**Tandem** — the multi-user co-conduct mode
**Sideband** — the private human backchannel within a Tandem session

Tagline candidate: "a confluence of voices" (confluence rejected as mode name — better as descriptor of the result)

---

## Rationale stubs

**Tandem:** [expand] Two operators, one vehicle, shared direction — neither is passenger, both are driving. Mechanical and clean. Legible without explanation. Fits CT's register of precision without pretension. Also: the asymmetry is already present in tandem — a tandem bicycle has a captain (front) and stoker (back); the captain steers, the stoker contributes power. Maps cleanly onto the Host/Partner role model.

**Sideband:** [expand] Radio/signal processing term — a frequency band adjacent to the main carrier, used for auxiliary transmission. Double meaning: literally runs beside the main session; also connotes the kind of communication that happens off to the side, between the principals, not for the record. That second meaning maps exactly onto the backchannel's function. The Sideband is not secret — both participants know it exists — but it is private to them and invisible to the models.

**Confluence rejected as mode name:** [expand] Too descriptive of outcome rather than structure. Better held as tagline — "a confluence of voices" names what Tandem produces, not what it is. Works as onboarding or marketing copy; would be vague as a UI label.

---

## Host / Partner role names

"Host" and "Partner" emerged during UI design session 2026-06-02. Functional, clear, asymmetric without being hierarchically loaded. "Teacher/Student" and "H1/H2" both considered and set aside — too specific to one use case (education) or too abstract (alphanumeric). Host/Partner covers the general case while remaining legible in the educational context.

## Narrative use

Teacher/student framing vs. peer co-conduct framing — same mode name, different narratives. The tandem bicycle metaphor may be useful for onboarding — captain steers, stoker contributes, both are moving together.
