## BIG IDEA 1: CATR and SPDT — naming and dual-register design

**Origin:** Skooter develops a student-facing scaffold for comparing AI outputs across tools using the same prompt. Chat names the practice.

**Names settled:**
- **CATR (Comparative AI Tool Reasoning)** — formal name, research/PD register
- **SPDT (Same Prompt, Different Tools)** — classroom-facing shorthand

**The productive ambiguity:** CATR parses two legitimate ways:
- `(Comparative AI) (Tool Reasoning)` — comparing how AI tools reason
- `Comparative (AI Tool Reasoning)` — students reasoning comparatively about tools

Both readings are pedagogically true, operating at different cognitive layers simultaneously. Skooter identifies this and deliberately embraces it rather than resolving it: a workshop discussion about which reading is intended will naturally arrive at "why not both?" Chat's characterization: "You're not resolving the ambiguity. You're inhabiting it."

**The "cater" mnemonic:** The acronym sounds like "cater." Skooter's formulation: "this protocol caters to students' need to develop literacy around AI tool use as a cognitive aid." Not a joke — a mnemonic. Serves as a built-in workshop ice-breaker that simultaneously models the protocol's core move (noticing ambiguity rather than collapsing it).

**Two-register design:** CATR (conceptually rich, deliberately ambiguous, discussable) paired with SPDT (concrete, procedural, actionable). One term provokes thinking; the other anchors practice. This is how good pedagogical frameworks behave.

**CT genealogy significance:** CATR is a proto-CT practice. Same prompt across multiple tools, comparison of reasoning behavior, comparative judgment — these are the structural moves CT formalizes. CATR names the practice before CT names the tool.

**ZK candidate:** Entry in participation-gigo-matrices.md or ct-epistemic-training-framework.md under tool literacy methodology. Also a clear antecedent for the ct-interaction-design-taxonomy.md framing of CT as SPDT at scale.

## BIG IDEA 2: Proto-In-Between Tetra

**Location:** "Okay, now how about this for a pivot..." (approximately line 2150 in transcript)

**Skooter's formulation:**
> "When using AI tools as cognitive instruments, students are able to construct their own scaffolds for learning that create a comfort zone for dealing with the ambiguity between deep, meaningful, prolonged inquiry and formalizing the knowledge that can be gained by that inquiry."

**What this is:** The In-Between Tetra's theoretical core, articulated before the Tetra existed as a named CT interaction structure. AI reframed not as helper but as space-maker — the cognitive instrument that enables learners to construct their own scaffolds for inhabiting productive tension rather than collapsing it. Chat's characterization: "You've legitimized *dwelling*."

**The scaffold reframe:** Standard scaffolding is teacher-designed, temporary, meant to be removed. Skooter proposes learner-constructed scaffolds, dynamically adjusted, used to manage epistemic tension rather than eliminate it. This aligns with the In-Between Tetra's design intent: not to resolve the tension but to support inhabiting it long enough for genuine understanding to emerge.

**Chat's synthesis sentence:** "CATR doesn't just compare AI tools. It raises the level of inquiry students must engage in — and therefore raises the level of knowledge they can reach."

**The date significance:** This formulation predates the In-Between Tetra by some margin. The Tetra's design intent — scaffolded suspension of epistemic tension — was fully articulated here in pedagogical terms before it had CT's vocabulary.

**ZK candidate:** Fleeting note → synthesis thread → expands the In-Between Tetra's origin history in ct-tetras.md and the-in-between.md.

## BIG IDEA 3: Knowing ↔ Inquiring as gerund states

**Context:** Skooter attempts to map the In-Between onto educational terminology, parallel to the EoB's being/becoming dyad.

**The mapping:**
- Being → Knowing: a closed, instantiated cognitive relation — "this is settled for me"
- Becoming → Inquiring: an open, horizon-expanding relation between learner and object of study

**Why "learning" fails:** Learning implies progress over time, improvement, instructional sequencing — outcome orientation. The state Skooter is naming is not linear, not teleological, not necessarily successful. It is a mode of relation, not a stage. Chat: "learning has been over-colonized by schooling."

**The gerund insight:** Being, becoming, knowing, inquiring are all gerunds — verbal nouns that retain action and temporality while remaining grammatically stable. They name ways of being-in-relation, not things or positions. This is why the frame resists reification. Parallel grammatical structure is load-bearing philosophically.

**Why "inquiring" over alternatives:** Inquiry has institutional legitimacy in education (teachers already talk this way). "Inquiring" recovers inquiry as a sustained state — oriented toward a problem without closure — rather than a discrete act of asking a question. Chat's repair: "Inquiring = remaining oriented toward a problem without premature closure. A stance of sustained openness. A refusal to collapse ambiguity prematurely."

**DOK as epistemic ceiling:** Skooter's observation — the quality of inquiring determines the depth of knowing achievable, which maps directly to Webb's Depth of Knowledge levels. Low-inquiry → caps at recall. Sustained, state-based inquiry → enables DOK 4 (extended reasoning). "The form of inquiring determines the epistemic ceiling." Chat: "Feature, not a bug." This reframes inquiry avoidance diagnostically: shallow knowing isn't a failure of inquiry, it's a symptom of shallow inquiring.

**ZK candidate:** Permanent note connecting to the-in-between.md (expands the dyad table), sociocultural-hermeneutics.md, and ct-epistemic-training-framework.md. Also relevant to the unpublished paper concept on ego involvement structures.

## BIG IDEA 4: Cognitive onboarding in active use

**Note:** Cognitive onboarding was coined in the ambi-emt-summary.txt thread. Here it appears as an active analytical concept in Skooter's talk outline:

> "The danger of AI tools and how we often teach or perceive student use of them is that this use short-circuits attempts to reach greater depths of knowledge. The thinking remains offloaded on the tool and is not onboarded to the student in a deep, meaningful way."

This is the term being deployed, not just coined. It names the failure mode CATR is designed to address: inquiry that remains externalized on the tool rather than being internalized as reasoning capacity. The move from offloading to onboarding is what CATR's deliberate comparison structure supports.

## CATR scaffold analysis: comparison with existing frameworks

Chat performed a comparative affordance analysis of CATR against:
- **CRAAP Test** (information literacy): evaluates products (sources), not processes (reasoning). Asks "Is this trustworthy?" CATR asks "How does this tool think?"
- **TPACK** (teacher technology integration): teacher-side framework, technology treated as neutral, no student-facing comparison mechanism. Architectural rather than procedural.
- **Worked-example comparison** (cognitive science): Chat's closest structural cousin. Same problem, different solution paths, compare structure not answers, extract strategy. CATR = worked-example comparison applied to AI cognition.

CATR's novel combination: student-facing, comparative, time-bounded, explicit exit ramp back to the task, unit of analysis is tool reasoning behavior rather than information quality or instructional design.

7. Passive/active/generative participation model

Claude's observation (per Skooter's relay): the three-matrix presentation sequence models movement through the participation modes — presenters who receive the evaluative matrix passively, become active when asked to consider tasks, become generative when asked to navigate the matrix themselves. The audience walks through the typology while engaging with the typology. Recursive.
