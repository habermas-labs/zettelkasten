Core concept: A dedicated Tetra configuration designed not for substantive output but for running controlled role experiments. Meta-CT — CT studying its own role mechanics. The container that the rotation conditions (ct-test-role-rotation-conditions.md) and priming experiments (ct-test-role-priming-cross-pollination.md) feed into as the experimental program matures.

Design requirements:
- Minimal session structure that isolates role variables
- Controlled topic (throwaway or standardized) to eliminate content as a confound
- Measurable output criteria: what counts as a behavioral delta?
- Condition comparison within a single session using CT's parallel architecture

Key design principle from triangulation session: CT's multi-model parallel architecture is the natural instrument for this research because it allows condition comparison within a session rather than across sessions, controlling for topic, conductor, and session context simultaneously. The paper writes itself as a methodological argument for CT alongside the empirical finding.

Open questions:
- What is the minimal role definition needed for valid experimental results? Too much scaffolding in the role prompt may contaminate the variable being tested.
- How do you operationalize "role integrity" as a measurable output? Possible metrics: number of objections raised, strength of adversarial pressure, resistance to conductor agreement-seeking.
- Can the testbed Tetra be run without a human conductor, or is the conductor's presence necessary to elicit the behaviors being tested?
- Does the testbed Tetra have a name? It is not an epistemic act in the Tetra taxonomy sense — it is a meta-level configuration. May require its own taxonomic category.

Fallback modes note: The testbed Tetra is also the natural context for developing fallback mode specifications for all roles. A role that cannot fulfill its epistemic function under constrained conditions (tool absence, rotation awareness, conductor absence) reveals its essential definition through what breaks. The testbed is where those breaks become visible and documentable.

General principle surfaced in session: every role definition should include a fallback mode specification — what the role becomes when its primary affordances are unavailable. For tool-dependent roles like Researcher this is obvious and necessary. For all roles it is worth asking. This is a Tetra design standard, not a per-role decision. Implement as a required field in role definition schema: primary function, epistemic act, fallback mode.
