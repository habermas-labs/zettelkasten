Origin: Gemini's "skill development for human participants" observation in the Fundraiser Tetra triangulation session (2026-03-13). Role rotation in CT-mediated sessions may produce learning benefits for human participants analogous to the documented benefits in Palincsar and Brown's Reciprocal Teaching — inhabiting a role builds genuine competency, not just performance of that competency.

Three distinct training questions (do not conflate):

1. Model training — how AI models learn roles, priming, cross-pollination effects on output quality. Experimental, testable. See ct-role-test-priming-cross-pollination.md.

2. Conductor training — the epistemic training framework already in progress (CT-082). How humans learn to orchestrate CT sessions effectively. Layer 2 knowledge: knowing how each model thinks, not just how to operate the interface.

3. Learner training — CT as a pedagogical instrument. Role rotation as metacognitive exercise. The human participant who inhabits the Skeptic role builds genuine critical evaluation skills, not just the performance of skepticism. This is the Reciprocal Teaching argument extended to the Fundraiser Tetra and beyond.

Pedagogical applications:
- Teacher-assisted assignment design: teachers use CT to create sessions that provide specific training opportunities for students. Metacognitive exercises structured around role rotation. Students inhabit each role across a sequence of sessions, building the cross-role perspective that improves all role performance.
- Grant writing training at professional level: the Fundraiser Tetra and Grant Writer Tetra as training instruments for professional development. A grant writer who has inhabited the Skeptic role understands funder objections from the inside. A Case-maker who has been the Translator understands audience register in a way that purely informational training cannot produce.
- The holy trinity analogy (gaming): knowing how the other roles function improves your own performance. A healer who has tanked anticipates tank needs rather than waiting to be told. Experiential cross-role knowledge transfers as anticipatory modeling — a different and more robust kind of competence than informational knowledge about other roles.

Research connection: Reciprocal Teaching (Palincsar and Brown, 1984) is already in the CT taxonomy (CT-072) because role rotation has documented learning benefits for comprehension. The Fundraiser Tetra extends that logic to strategic persuasion and professional skill development. The underlying principle is general: roles designed to scaffold a cognitive act build genuine competency in that act when inhabited, not merely when observed.

Open questions:
- Does the learning benefit require genuine role inhabitation (active generation) or is observation sufficient? Reciprocal Teaching evidence suggests inhabitation is necessary.
- How does the CT multi-model architecture change the learning dynamic compared to human-only role rotation? The models provide consistent, high-quality role modeling that human peers may not — a potential advantage for learners inhabiting adjacent roles.
- What is the right granularity for role rotation in a pedagogical context? Palincsar used turn-by-turn rotation; CT sessions may require longer role durations for genuine inhabitation.

Paper potential: CT as empirical substrate for studying role-based learning in human-AI collaborative sessions. Connects education research, cognitive psychology, and HCI. The methodology argument and the empirical finding are separable and both publishable.
