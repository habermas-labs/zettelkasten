Core question: Is AI role integrity under rotation conditions compromised by a ghost of ego-involvement operating through heteroglossia compression, or is the AI genuinely ego-free in the relevant sense?

Three-condition experimental design:
1. Skeptic with no knowledge of rotation (baseline)
2. Skeptic informed rotation will occur at indeterminate future point
3. Skeptic informed rotation occurs in two turns

Predicted human pattern: gradient of drift across conditions, strongest skepticism in condition 1, weakest in 3 — imminence amplifying consistency bias and face-saving. AI should not exhibit this gradient if ego-absence is structural. If gradient appears, it is a ghost of ego operating through training compression of human discourse patterns, not ego itself.

Programmatic intervention to test: instruct Skeptic role explicitly to argue its position as if permanent and unconditional regardless of rotation. If this instruction produces a behavioral delta, the ghost is real and addressable in role definition. If no delta, AI is already ego-free in this sense.

Gemini's contribution from triangulation session 2026-03-13: blind or random rotation eliminates the anticipation variable entirely — models that don't know rotation is coming cannot proactively pull punches. This is condition 1 and Gemini independently derived why it's the right baseline. Also: the "pulling punches vs. informed perspective" distinction names the mechanism precisely. The problem isn't rotation itself, it's anticipation contaminating current performance. Cross-pollination benefit only accrues if current role performs with full integrity first — sequential, not simultaneous.

Secondary finding regardless of outcome: prompt sensitivity to information about a model's own future state is interesting independent of the ego question — speaks to how models weight self-situational context.

CT as instrument and demonstration case: the multi-model parallel architecture allows condition comparison within a single session. The paper writes itself as a methodological argument for CT alongside the empirical finding.

Relevant literature to locate: consistency bias research, role-playing and identity in LLMs, sycophancy as structural response (connects to existing CT theory), ego depletion literature as contrast case.
