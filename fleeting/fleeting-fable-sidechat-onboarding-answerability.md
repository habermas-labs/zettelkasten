## 2. Human-side Gricean failure modes (workload outsourcing extended)

Fable's analysis, prompted by Skooter's question about whether the field locates AI failure modes exclusively as architectural/procedural problems rather than human-communicative ones:

- **Existing literature is real, but wrong register.** Automation bias, Parasuraman & Riley's misuse/disuse/abuse taxonomy, Bainbridge's ironies of automation, and offloading/academic-integrity framing all treat the failing human as a miscalibrated *operator* (control-systems framing), never as a failing *communicator* (pragmatic framing). **New bibliography candidates** for theoretical-sources-annotated.md: Parasuraman & Riley, Bainbridge — currently absent from the vault's bibliography and directly useful as the "what HAC is positioned against" literature.
- **Speaker-side:** underspecified prompts ("write my paper on the Civil War") are Quantity violations that get charged to the model as hallucination/misalignment when they're actually malformed contributions. Deeper still — the real Quality violation in the outsourcing case is in the H→H dyad (submitting the paper asserts sincerity conditions to the teacher the student cannot satisfy); the H→AI exchange is just the manufacturing site. Single prompt-response analysis is looking in the wrong dyad entirely.
- **Hearer-side — two candidate new terms, not yet in the vault's terminology:** *cooperative free-riding* (taking the products of cooperation while contributing none of the hearer's inferential labor) and *misapplied cooperative presumption* (extending the human presumption that interlocutors track belief — Quality — to a system whose calibration is not sincerity, since it has no beliefs). Automation bias reframed Griceanly as a category error about what kind of interlocutor is on the other end.

## 3. Cognitive onboarding — new theoretical grounding (not new to the vault, but newly grounded)

Cognitive onboarding already exists in the vault (fleeting-arch-ttt-emt-summary.md, origin thread) as the reciprocal of offloading. This side-chat adds real load-bearing grounding that wasn't there before:

- **Otto's-notebook flip (Clark & Chalmers, amended):** automatic endorsement is the criterion that *qualifies* Otto's notebook as extended mind — he doesn't interrogate it, just believes it, because it's a static store of his own past cognition. Applied to a *generative* system the valence flips: automatic endorsement of an LLM output is endorsing an alien prior, not oneself, so the very criterion that licenses extended-mind status for a notebook is what disqualifies uncritical AI use as learning. This gives workload-outsourcing.md a sharper philosophical mechanism than it currently has for why Extended Mind doesn't rescue the naive "it's just extending my mind" defense — onboarding becomes the *replacement* for automatic endorsement when the resource is generative rather than storative.
- **Wertsch's mastery vs. appropriation** (refinement of Vygotskian internalization): the outsourcing student achieves neither. Gives the onboarding quality gradient a named source.
- **Porosity Tetra's Witness, reread:** its stated function — discriminating real accommodation (schema restructuring) from new material reprocessed in existing terms — is exactly onboarding's quality metric (Piagetian accommodation vs. disguised assimilation), designed for a different Tetra before the term "onboarding" existed. Worth a cross-reference note in ct-tetras.md / hedra-facing-presets.md.
- **Wittrock's generative learning theory (1974) + Fiorella & Mayer's *Learning as a Generative Activity* (2015):** comprehension requires learner-side generation (self-explaining, teaching back, summarizing in one's own words, mapping, enacting); model-side generation in "Generative AI" produces zero learning until matched by this. This makes "generative in truth" a technically precise reclamation, not wordplay, and hands onboarding a concrete behavioral battery. **Three independent coding schemes now converge on the same construct:** Herbert Clark's acceptance-phase (below), Kember's reflection levels (already in vault, per the earlier Fable exchange this transcript continues from), and Fiorella & Mayer's generative strategies. Worth naming as triangulated instrumentation if this ever goes into a fellowship document.
- **Herbert Clark's contribution theory** (*Using Language*, 1996) — genuinely new to the vault's bibliography. The *contribution* (presentation phase + acceptance phase, completed only when the hearer provides evidence of understanding; grounding accumulates as common ground) is a formal alternative unit of analysis to the single prompt-response dyad — exactly what the four-dyads work (ego-mimicry-taxonomy-and-hedra-design.md) was reaching for without naming. Uncritical acceptance = a degenerate acceptance phase, a countable, checkable construct. **Candidate for its own bibliography entry and a cross-reference from the four-dyads permanent note when it's drafted.**
- **Two structural closers, worth preserving near-verbatim if this gets developed:** (1) offloading and onboarding are coupled — Quantity-starved offloads produce onboarding the student lacks context to evaluate, but genuine onboarding enriches the next offload, which is what makes the loop a spiral rather than a cycle; (2) CT's no-persistence architecture *is* the subtraction test run temporally — when the session evaporates, the distributed system's common ground is destroyed and whatever was onboarded into the human is the only surviving residue. This is not a new claim so much as a sharp restatement connecting workload-outsourcing.md, kirsh-maglio-epistemic-actions.md (subtraction test), and ct-fleeting-session-knowledge-architecture.md (session volatility as epistemic discipline) — three existing notes this single sentence ties together.

## 4. Heteroglossia, addressivity, answerability

- **Two-stage compression, sharper than the vault's current single-stage claim.** heteroglossia-compression-ai-provenance.md and related notes currently treat "LLMs are centripetal" as one undifferentiated compression effect. Fable's version separates two stages: pretraining ingests centrifugal diversity (the heteroglossia, unstratified); alignment is a distinct, later centripetal force imposing the unitary assistant voice on top of that. Consequence: when models push back on the heteroglossia framing ("an AI can't have heteroglossia, it isn't living in those utterances"), that denial is itself an artifact of the alignment-stage centripetal force, not testimony about the pretrained substrate underneath. **This is a real refinement, not restatement — worth folding into heteroglossia-compression-ai-provenance.md as an explicit two-stage model.**
- **Bakhtin's answerability — genuinely absent from the vault.** For Bakhtin the voice is anchored in an author who signs the act and can be held to account; compressed voices arrive orphaned — addressivity without answerability. Candidate resolution offered in-transcript: the conductor is the one who signs the session. This is a direct, missing piece for ct-input-layer-user-agency.md, which already treats the conductor as Bakhtinian author but doesn't yet have the answerability vocabulary for *why* that authorial function is load-bearing rather than a nice metaphor.
- **Superaddressee.** Bakhtin's superaddressee (the ideally-understanding third party every utterance also orients toward) maps directly onto Witness/Silent Observer roles as superaddressees given actual vertices, and the diachronic Observer as the superaddressee given *time* — the future reader every exported transcript implicitly addresses. Worth a line in whichever note eventually formalizes the diachronic Observer role, if that role gets its own permanent note.
- **The relational-ontology closer** — worth preserving close to verbatim as candidate front-matter for a fellowship document: heteroglossia lives in the instantiation between; meaning comes to fruition only in the response between; mind develops between people before within them; transitional space sits between inner and outer; the tetrahedron's value is in its edges, not its vertices. Offloading and onboarding are simply the two directed edges of that relational ontology.
