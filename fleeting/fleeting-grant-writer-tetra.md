Raw capture from walking session.

- **Epistemic act:** Institutional persuasion — constructing an evidential, criteria-driven argument for capital from a formal review body. Distinct from public fundraising in register, structure, and audience expectations.
- **CT positioning advantage:** The existing theoretical framework (ZK, vocabulary, Chowdhury resonances, educational research grounding) is precisely what grant reviewers want to see. CT has receipts most indie developers lack. The framework is not just the product — it is the grant application.
- **Tentative role set:** Not yet developed — needs its own session. Likely includes roles oriented around criteria-matching, evidential argumentation, and reviewer-perspective simulation. Distinct from Fundraiser roles despite shared capital goal.
- **Partner relationship to Fundraiser Tetra** (see companion note): Functional partners, not geometric mirrors. Same underlying need (capital), different rhetorical worlds. Public-facing emotional persuasion (Fundraiser) vs. institutional evidential argument (Grant Writer).
- **Shared vertex — Researcher role:** The Researcher role defined in the Fundraiser Tetra may be portable here — finding grant opportunities is the same outward-facing gesture as finding crowdfunding matches, aimed at institutional rather than community targets. If confirmed, establishes the concept of portable roles across Tetras as a taxonomy-level property.
